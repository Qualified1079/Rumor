Rumor is a messenger that runs entirely on the devices using it. There is
no server, no account, no phone number. Two phones running Rumor talk
directly to each other over Bluetooth or Wi-Fi Direct. If a third phone is
nearby, it relays messages between phones that can't reach each other
directly — your message hops device to device until it arrives.

This means Rumor keeps working when the internet doesn't: power outages,
crowded events that overload cell towers, areas without coverage, or
networks that have been deliberately cut. As long as there are phones
running Rumor within radio range, messages move.

Features:

- Direct messages and broadcasts to nearby contacts
- Group "Rooms" — text channels you can join, leave, or create
- Bridges to existing radio networks (Meshtastic, MeshCore)
- File transfer with pause and resume
- Plugin support for extensions like content filters and bridges
- End-to-end encryption on every direct message (X25519 + AES-GCM,
  Ed25519 signatures)
- No tracking, no telemetry, no third-party services

What Rumor is not:

- Not anonymous against an adversary in radio range. Anyone who can hear
  Bluetooth can see that your device is broadcasting, the same way they
  can see any other Bluetooth device.
- Not a replacement for Signal when you have internet. Rumor's strength
  is working without infrastructure.
- Not a guarantee of delivery. Messages reach as far as the mesh extends.

Rumor is free and open source. Built to work without depending on any
particular company, server, or piece of infrastructure.
